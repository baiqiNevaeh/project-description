Nudging Interpretation and Development of Privacy Policies

Research Statement

Privacy concerns about personal data have raised high attention among online end-users in recent years. Many countries publish their data protection regulations to regulate online products' behavior, including disclosing collected data types for data collection and listing trusted third parties for data sharing. In response, these products' developers are required to release a privacy policy document for their users to declare the data handling practices. Privacy policies are regarded as legal or semi-legal documents, which are usually lengthy and complex for users to understand.

Users might skip them before using an online product. As a result, users use the online product without knowing their rights and the products' behavior, including what data the products will collect. Once any data breach occurs, users could be easily put in an unfair situation in disputes.

Additionally, due to the complexity of privacy policies, developers may unintentionally include vague or misleading statements for their data handling behaviors, or miss statements. In this situation, developers will violate the data protection regulations and mislead users' understanding of their products.

Recent Publications

  • Investigating Users' Understanding of Privacy Policies of Virtual Personal Assistant Applications

    Chen, Baiqi and Wu, Tingmin and Zhang, Yanjun and Chhetri, Mohan Baruwal and Bai, Guangdong
    ACM ASIA Conference on Computer and Communications Security (ASIA CCS), 2023

    Project Descriptions:
    We have conducted a subjective study to investigate users' comprehension of the privacy policies, taking as our target skills of Amazon Alexa, the most popular virtual personal assistant (VPA) service. Our study focuses on the effect of the words, phrases and jargons (summarized as technical terms), the common hurdles of users' understanding. We find that most online users have difficulties in understanding the Alexa Skills' privacy policies, and the technical terms are the important factor for their understanding. We then propose two principles for creating explanations and then conduct a user study to evaluate the effectiveness of the explanation sentences composed based on our principles.
